What does it mean?

To meet the challenges of today's complex cyber-attacks, U.S.T.A. is reinforced with dozens of intelligence collection tools that monitor thousands of different sources.

As each cyber threat type requires a different monitoring approach. USTAs tracking tools and intelligence sources vary according to different types of threat-including but not limited to:

  • Targeted attacks against USTA member organizations such as advanced persistent threats, zero-day vulnerabilities, and spear-phishing campaigns.

  • Targeted and generic attacks against clients of USTA member organizations such as Malware-as-a-Service and Ransomware-as-a-Service campaigns, stealer botnets, or phishing sites

  • Out-of-band attacks, such as malicious social media campaigns or fake mobile applications, that damage the reputations of USTA members

  • Generic/indirect attacks against USTA member organizations such as ransomware threats, phishing campaigns, and stealer malware
